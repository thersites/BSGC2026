Each country receives a composite AI presence score from 0 to 100, where 0 means virtually no AI presence and 100 means fully AI-saturated. The composite is the simple average of three equally-weighted dimensions:

Daily Life AI (33%)

Measures how much AI consumers encounter in everyday life — smart assistants, recommendation engines, facial recognition in public spaces, and AI-driven services in retail, healthcare, and transport.

Industry AI (33%)

Measures AI adoption across sectors such as manufacturing, finance, agriculture, and logistics. Reflects both the number of AI deployments and their economic footprint.

Surveillance AI (33%)

Measures the extent of government and commercial AI-powered surveillance: CCTV networks, biometric databases, social-scoring systems, and predictive policing tools.

Data Sources

  • Oxford Insights Government AI Readiness Index — annual readiness assessment for 193 countries
  • Stanford HAI AI Index — tracks AI investment, research output, and adoption by country
  • OECD AI Policy Observatory — policy and deployment data for OECD and partner nations

Scores are curated estimates based on publicly available indices. Countries without sufficient data are shown in grey and excluded from rankings.
